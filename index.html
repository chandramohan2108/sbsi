Goal of the internship

The 100 hour experiential internship program would require the candidate to visit one or more villages and conduct activities of their choice to contribute to the cause of rural sanitation in India.

Prime Minister's clarion call on 2nd October 2014 for each individual to devote 100 hours to Swachhata through the year,the Ministry of Human Resource Developmentin association with the Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation has launched the'Swachh Bharat Summer Internship â€“ 100 Hours of Swachhata'. The program aims to ensure th e engagement of lakhs of educated youth across the country, develop their skill and orientation for sanitation related work, amplify mass awareness of Swachh Bharat Mission and cement connectivity between the mission and the people at large.

Activities under Swachh Bharat Summer Internship

As a part of the internship, the activities that participants may conduct have been categorized into two clusters

Information-Education-Communication activities

  • Awareness Campaignstowards better sanitation practices.
  • Swachhata related Nukkad Nataks/ Street Plays.
  • Organization of Swachhata Melas or Swachhata-related Folk Song and Dance performances.
  • Door-to-door meetingswith delivery of behaviour change messages with respect to use of toilets.
  • Conducting Village or School-level Rallies to generate awareness about sanitation.
  • Wall Paintings on public walls in public places, andgovernmentbuildings like Panchayat Ghar(displaying the message of sanitation, and making public the list of beneficiaries in the village covered underSwachh Bharat Mission).
  • Organizing movie screenings: Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) movies may be found on the YouTube channel tinyurl.com/sbmgramin and community screenings of these films may be organized in public places in the villages to help spread the message of Swachhata.
  • All IEC Material of Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) may be freely downloaded from the links tinyurl.com/sbmiec and tinyurl.com/sbmiec2. Any other activities undertaken to help spread these messages to the rural masses would count under the header of IEC activities.

Solid Waste Management related activities:

  • Waste Collection Drives (households/ common or shared spaces).
  • Segregation of solid wasteinto non-biodegradable and biodegradable waste.
  • Development of Compost Pits (where organic matter decomposes/ decays to create manure).
  • Transportation of Household waste (to appropriate disposal site where it can be treated or from where it can be sent to a treatment site easily).
  • Helping Panchayats draw up plans for installation of biogas plants wherever possible/required.
  • Street Cleaning, Drain Cleaning, Cleaning of back alleys.

  • All participants will be given a Swachh Bharat Internship Certificate upon successful completion.
  • 2 curriculum credits will be given by institutions affiliated with UGC to all interns.
  • Best internships will be recognized at college, university, state and national levels
